Binary Search

Binary search is a much more efficient searching algorithm than linear search, but has the unfortunate caveat of requiring the list to be in order. It works by repeatedly checking the middle item, and taking half of the list depending on whether the item to be found is bigger or smaller than the middle item. Binary search has a time complexity of O(log n) and Ω(1).
